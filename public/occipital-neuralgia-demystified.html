Occipital Neuralgia Demystified: Expert Insights on Symptoms, Causes, and Treatment Options


Expert Guidance from Dr. Arun Reddy M, Leading Neurosurgeon and Endoscopic Spine Surgeon at Indi Spine-Brain Neuro Care, the Premier Neuro Clinic in Madinaguda, Hyderabad"

The occipital nerves, originating at the top of the spinal column and coursing through the head, can lead to occipital neuralgia when irritated or damaged. This condition manifests as pain at the base of the skull or the back of the head, often misinterpreted as a migraine or headache. Consulting Dr. Arun Reddy M at Indi Spine-Brain Neuro Care is essential for accurate diagnosis and tailored treatment.

Occipital Neuralgia: Is it Curable? While not conventionally curable, occipital neuralgia can be effectively managed and attacks prevented through proper care. Identifying and treating the underlying causes contribute to successful outcomes.

Symptoms of Occipital Neuralgia:

  • Rapid-fire headache discomfort.
  • Persistent, dull headache.
  • Sensitivity to light.
  • Throbbing, aching, or burning pain radiating from the scalp to the base of the head.
  • Pain on one or both sides of the head.
  • Eye pain.
  • Soft scalp.
  • Discomfort during neck movement.
Causes of Occipital Neuralgia: Occipital neuralgia arises when the occipital nerve roots, located at the top of the spinal cord, become inflamed or irritated. Various factors contribute, including injury, muscle tightness, infections, diabetes, tumors, and more.

Treatment Options:
  • Massage treatment for muscle relaxation.
  • Over-the-counter medications for pain and inflammation.
  • Prescription drugs like muscle relaxants or antidepressants.
  • Injections for targeted pain relief.


Physical therapy to address muscle stress and poor posture. Home remedies, including warm compress, self-massage, and green tea. Surgery, if other interventions prove ineffective.
