Endoscopic spine surgery is an innovative surgical technique that allows for the diagnosis and treatment of various spine conditions through small incisions using an endoscope.

This minimally invasive approach offers numerous advantages over traditional open surgery, including reduced tissue damage, faster recovery times, and less postoperative pain.

At Indi Spine-Brain Neuro Care, the best spine and neuro clinic in Madinaguda, we specialize in performing endoscopic spine surgeries to provide effective treatment for a wide range of spinal disorders.

Advantages of Endoscopic Spine Surgery:

  • Minimally Invasive: Endoscopic spine surgery utilizes small incisions, typically less than one inch, which minimizes the disruption of surrounding muscles and tissues. This approach results in less scarring and reduced trauma to the body.
  • Reduced Blood Loss: The smaller incisions used in endoscopic surgery lead to reduced blood loss during the procedure, lowering the need for transfusions and decreasing the risk of complications.
  • Faster Recovery: Compared to traditional open surgery, endoscopic procedures offer faster recovery times. The smaller incisions and reduced tissue damage allow for quicker healing and less postoperative pain. Patients can often return to their daily activities sooner.
  • Improved Visualization: The endoscope, a thin, flexible tube with a camera and light source, provides high-definition images of the surgical site. This enhanced visualization enables surgeons to perform precise maneuvers while minimizing damage to surrounding structures.
  • Targeted Treatment: Endoscopic spine surgery allows surgeons to access specific areas of the spine with precision. It enables them to address the root cause of the problem while preserving healthy tissues and minimizing the risk of complications.


Common Endoscopic Spine Surgeries:

  • Endoscopic Discectomy: This procedure is performed to treat herniated discs. The surgeon removes the herniated portion of the disc through a small incision using specialized instruments. This relieves pressure on the nerves and alleviates pain.
  • Endoscopic Foraminotomy: It is used to treat foraminal stenosis, a condition where the nerve roots exiting the spinal canal are compressed. The surgeon removes the bone or tissue causing the compression, widening the foraminal space and relieving nerve compression.
  • Endoscopic Spinal Fusion: This procedure involves the fusion of two or more vertebrae using bone grafts or spinal implants. It is performed to stabilize the spine and alleviate pain caused by conditions such as degenerative disc disease or spinal instability.
